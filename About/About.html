What is TSA?



TSA (Technology Student Association) is a national organization dedicated to creating future leaders in the science, technology, engineering, and mathematics fields. The organization uses its yearly set of competitions to test the abilities of the students competing in the event. In addition to expanding their knowledge, the members are able to represent their school and community when competing.

The TSA motto “learning to lead in a technical world” is precisely what the organization is working towards, as the club has opened branches in 48 of the 50 states. The overall mission of TSA is to have the members of the club learn and develop their skills in the STEM department and to eventually implement them on solutions towards the world’s most concerning problems.

Mission

The Technology Student Association (TSA) enhances personal development, leadership, and career opportunities in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM), whereby members apply and integrate these concepts through intracurricular activities, competitions, and related programs.



Learn More
Vision

TSA is accelerating student achievement and supporting teachers by providing engaging opportunities to develop STEM skills. TSA members have the opportunity to compete at state conferences and at the annual National Conference. Expert judging by technology educators and industry representatives inspires the best from participants. Members are awarded not only with medals and trophies but also with memories of innovation and collaboration.

Rock Ridge TSA



Our History

We are Rock Ridge TSA. Located at Ashburn, Loudoun County, Rock Ridge is home to masses of students dedicated to STEM. Realizing that all of this passion and talent of our fellow classmates could be used to better the community and promote collaboration, Rock Rock TSA was created. With over 7 years of consistent TSA participation and placement in competitons, Rock Ridge High School has established itself as a strong and competent chapter.


Our TSA chapter works closely with an FTC team that competed this past April of 2019 for world championship. These individuals have teamed up for multiple years, and have finally made it to the biggest stage to display their innovation, collaboration, and creativity. In addition to that, Rock Ridge has partnered with a secondary STEM school in the county, the Academies of Loudoun. They host weekly plant sales and flower festivals to celebrate accomplishments that students have made in their program at the Academies of Loudoun.

Meetings


Attendance

To ensure consistent participation in the club, mandatory attendance forms are filled by club members every weekly meeting. With this information, important information relevant to the approaching competitions and community projects are relayed accordingly. This year we had amazing participation and commitment with a 95% attendance rate!

Meeting Agenda

To keep all students intact with their TSA event, the club runs weekly meetings to regroup the teams and update each other on any group progress. The club officers begin the meetings with recaps on competition dates, event updates, and general announcements. Throughout the meetings, teams meet up to share their personal progress on the project and plan out future progress.

Involvement


Guest Speakers

To inspire the students and expose them to other applications of technology, the President of the chapter asked for a few guests to speak at one of the weekly meetings. The guests were a self initiated FIRST FTC robotics team and their mentor, who demoed their robot and spoke about their programming design.


Party

To celebrate New Years, the students held a party at one of the weekly meetings in January. The party became a quick success as all the members of the club had brought different food items to contribute. Students shared their projects with other groups, enjoying themselves and each other's company.

Community


Future Coders of America

Realizing the increasing signifance and impact of computer programming and technology in our lives, TSA chapter members, namely Akhilesh Sivaganesan and Arjun Trivedi, created a separate club named Future Coders of America (FCA). FCA is an after school club that gathers high school students currently enrolled in Computer Science courses to tutor middle and elementary school students on the fundamentals of coding. The club works very closely with TSA and often TSA members are also members of FCA. FCA offers free classes that cover Java and Python through simple lessons hosted by nearby libraries and local spaces like the Curie Learning Center and Brambleton Library. Rock Ridge TSA is proud to spread digital literacy among our younger population.

Engineering

The Rock Ridge Engineering Program allows for students to engage in an intensive study of engineering while practicing the Engineering Design process. Rock Ride Engineering is inquiry-based with much of the content learned through investigation and problem-based exercises. Students will practice engineering fundamentals, using mathematical and scientific concepts, and they will apply the engineering design process through participation in hands-on engineering projects. Students communicate project-related information through team-based presentations, proposals, and technical reports.
